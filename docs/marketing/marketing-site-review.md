## Marketing site — review checklist

This is the studio’s internal checklist for the public marketing site.

**Purpose:** keep marketing short, clear, and legally precise — without internal jargon.

---

## Surfaces (routes)

- `/` — positioning and primary CTAs
- `/templates` — catalog; fast scan; no over-selling
- `/templates/[id]` — template detail; demo links; start brief
- `/pricing` — tiers + scope truth strip; “orienting, not binding”
- `/how-we-work` — definitions, phases, boundaries before signing
- `/contact` — intake; minimal friction; clear expectations
- `/case-studies` — reference builds; outcomes framing without client logos
- `/privacy`, `/terms` — readable, not essay-length
- `/status` — diagnostics (footer link only; not primary nav)

---

## Global rules

- **One primary CTA** on every page: “Start a project” → `/contact?intent=project`
- **No internal jargon** on public pages (kickoff, milestones, portal scopes, etc.)
- **Every promise is qualified**: proposal/SOW is binding; marketing is not
- **Pages are skimmable**: headings, short paragraphs, no walls of text
- **Demos are labeled as demos**: sample data, not production environments

---

## Content checks (per page)

### Home (`/`)
- Clear “what we do” in the first 10 seconds.
- Secondary CTA helps self-qualification (templates, pricing, how we work).
- No pricing figures in hero.

### Templates (`/templates`, `/templates/[id]`)
- “Shipped” vs “beta” explained in one line.
- If capacity closed, the waitlist CTA is the only primary action.
- Template detail answers: “what is it”, “who is it for”, “what ships”, “what’s next”.

### Pricing (`/pricing`)
- Tiers map to “invention level” not hours.
- Truth strip is visible without scrolling deep.
- Each tier CTA lands on the correct prefilled contact variant.

### How we work (`/how-we-work`)
- Jump links to: Before you sign, Scope model, Glossary, Phases.
- “Go-live” definition is precise and repeats the “proposal wins” rule.

### Contact (`/contact`)
- Autofilled context visible to the operator but not overwhelming to clients.
- Confirmation page has one crisp next step + expected response time.
- Spam/rate limits don’t block legitimate leads.

### Work (`/case-studies`)
- Explains why logos aren’t shown (permission) and what is shown instead (reference builds).
- Adds outcomes framing (what’s shipped, what it proves), not just templates.

---

## Review cadence

- After any change to templates/pricing/navigation: run a full click-through and review this checklist.
- Before a demo: verify the “Start a project” flow end to end.
