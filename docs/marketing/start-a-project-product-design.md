## Start a project — product design (outside-in)

This is the “what should it actually be” view for Goldspire Studio’s intake and delivery system.

It is written from multiple hats: product, sales, delivery, legal, finance, and operations.

---

## 1) What we are actually selling (in plain terms)

Clients are not buying “a form” or “a template”. They are buying:

- **A credible release** (their market can use it) on a fixed boundary,
- **An accountable delivery process** (predictable, visible, change-controlled),
- **A handover they can own** (code + infra + runbooks), and
- Optional: **a care plan** (retainer) once they’re live.

The intake system must turn uncertainty into a signed boundary without turning the studio into a custom agency.

---

## 2) The failure modes we must design against

These are why studios fail even with good engineering:

- **Unqualified leads flood the team**: response becomes slow, reputation suffers.
- **Scope ambiguity**: “yes” is interpreted as “everything”.
- **Silent change orders**: operators bend, margin dies.
- **Client accounts gate launch**: stores/Stripe/legal delays become “studio failure”.
- **Status anxiety**: clients ask for updates because the system doesn’t show progress.
- **No closure discipline**: handover is incomplete; support becomes endless.

The solution is not more words on marketing pages — it’s a lifecycle with explicit gates and automation.

---

## 3) What “Start a project” should be (the product)

### A) A single, canonical “Engagement Thread”

One thread persists from first click to close-out. It contains:

- the brief + provenance (template/tier/sku/intents)
- qualification and next questions
- the studio’s written reply and recommendation
- discovery artefacts (when used)
- proposal and SOW snapshot
- portal acceptance and payments (when used)
- kickoff intake
- delivery updates (client-facing)
- handover checklist + repository transfer confirmation
- retainer decision + next follow-up date (if any)

This thread is the studio’s memory and the client’s confidence.

### B) Two experiences, one truth

- **Client experience**: short, calm, obvious next steps.
- **Operator experience**: structured fields, gates, automation, no guesswork.

Both experiences read from the same underlying data.

---

## 4) The ideal lifecycle (how it should flow)

### Step 1 — Submit brief (2 minutes)

Minimum viable client input:
- who is it for + what problem
- geography + distribution (web, mobile, both)
- monetisation intent
- timeline + budget band
- template/tier context (prefilled)

### Step 2 — Auto-triage (instant)

System decides **what happens next** (suggested, not forced):
- Not a fit → send a polite decline template
- Needs discovery → propose discovery sprint
- Fit for clone/template/blueprint → propose a call + proposal timeline

### Step 3 — Written reply (within SLA)

The studio’s reply must include:
- “Here’s the path we recommend”
- “Here’s what would be in scope (high level)”
- “Here’s what is explicitly not included unless signed”
- “Here’s what we need from your side (accounts)”
- a next step (book call / answer questions / accept discovery)

### Step 4 — Proposal (structured, fast)

Proposal is generated from structured scope fields, then edited.
Client accepts in portal and pays the first milestone if scoped.

### Step 5 — Delivery (visible)

Client sees:
- staging URL
- next demo time
- milestone progress
- update timeline

### Step 6 — Close-out (checklist, not vibes)

Handover is a checklist. Closure includes:
- repo access transfer
- infra keys transfer
- runbooks delivered
- retainer decision recorded

---

## 5) Automation recommendations (where to be aggressive)

- **Routing + SLA alerts**: no lead sits silently.
- **Qualification gates**: conversion requires explicit minimum info.
- **Proposal generation**: templates + tier presets are built-in.
- **Portal**: acceptance + payments + kickoff intake + client updates.
- **Handover**: checklist completion gates “close”.

---

## 6) Success metrics (north star for intake)

- Median time from “Start a project” click → submitted brief: **< 2 minutes**
- Median time from lead created → first human reply: **< 4 business hours**
- Conversion from “fit leads” → signed proposal: **measured and improving**
- “Where are we?” client pings: **decreasing** (status is visible)
- Handover completeness: **100%** (checklist, not memory)
